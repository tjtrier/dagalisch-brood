Why I Bake

Honestly? Baking saved me. There's something about the rhythm of it — the measuring, the folding, the waiting — that quiets the noise in a way little else does. When my hands are in dough, my mind finally settles. It has become one of my most consistent forms of peace, a daily practice that grounds me and gives the day a sense of purpose.

But what started as something I did for myself quickly became something I wanted to share. There is a particular joy in handing someone a loaf of bread that was made that morning — watching them smell it, feel the weight of it, knowing they're about to experience something most people never get from a grocery store. That moment never gets old for me. It genuinely fills me up.

I bake because I believe real food should be made from real ingredients by real people. My bread contains flour, water, starter, and Himalayan sea salt — nothing else. The process takes longer, a proper sourdough fermentation runs 12 to 24 hours, but the result is a loaf that is more flavorful, more digestible, and more honest than anything made in a factory. I refuse to compromise on that.

Part of the joy is in the living culture itself. I maintain several starters with different origins — one from Egypt named "Esther," one from France called "Frenchie," and one from Italy known simply as "Italian." Many people say that if you feed starters the same flour they eventually become identical, but I find each one develops its own personality, aroma, and rhythm. Learning to read those differences is one of the most fascinating parts of this craft.

I also bake because I love this community. In a world that often feels chaotic and disconnected, something as simple as fresh bread can still bring people together. Offering military, first responder, and senior discounts isn't a marketing tactic — it's simply the right thing to do. Good bread should be accessible to the people who have given so much.

At the end of the day, baking is my small way of blessing people — sharing something warm, handmade, and offered with genuine care. That's enough for me.
